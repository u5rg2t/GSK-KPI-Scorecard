Sales growth

Reported monthly

Our sales growth is faster than the market

Measured as net sales performance vs plan year-to-date (YTD)

To deliver this we have to beat the competition as our plan for sales growth is higher than underlying market growth
  • Either equal to or better than plan YTD
  • 99%of plan YTD
  • Less than 99% of plan YTD

Operating profit

Reported monthly

We deliver faster growth in operating profitability than the market

Measured as operating profit performance vs plan YTD

To deliver this we have to grow more profitably than the competition
  • Equal to or better than plan YTD
  • Between 98% and plan YTD
  • Below 98% of plan YTD

Working capital

Reported monthly

Through delivering efficiencies, reduce the cash cost of our business to compete with the leading players in our industry

Measured as actual investment in working capital vs plan, as a number of days

It totals our days invested in inventory and accounts receivable, less days of credit negotiated with suppliers
  • Equal to or less than plan at quarter end
  • Up to 3 days more than the plan
  • In excess of 3 days higher than the plan

Market share

Reported monthly

Our brands grow consumption value at a higher rate than the category, thereby growing ahead of the competition

Measured as the difference between GSK CH's growth and the overall category's (i.e. total market, including both GSK and Competitor) growth.

Can be done at total GSK CH, category, brand, and geographical levels/breaks.
  • GSK is growing at a faster rate than the category, so we are growing market share
  • GSK is growing at same rate than category so we are maintaining market share
  • GSK is growing at lower rate than the category so we are losing market share

Brand equity

Reported monthly

We create strong desire within our target consumer to choose our brand over the competitors'

Brand equity is the measured value of consumer attitudes and beliefs about a brand against key competitors'.

It's calculated using 6 factors: Familiarity, Relevancy, Uniqueness, Quality, Popularity and Substitutability.

The Equity is an index where less than 80=below average against our competitors, 80 to 120= average, more than 120 = above average
  • Stronger than key competitor(s)
  • Same as key competitor(s)
  • Weaker than key competitor(s)

Channel Share

Reported monthly

We have the fastest share growth across Mass Market / Pharmacy channels for the previous 3 months

Generally, Mass Market consists of FMCG brands sold by grocery and other general format retailers, whilst Pharmacy retails our OTC brands that are not normally available - or not a focus in - the Mass Market channel. The split may vary by local market regulations.

The source varies by market (often IRL Nielsen data) and is a bespoke monthly calculation weighted by the local brand portfolio within each channel.

At the market level:

  • GSK growth faster than category
  • GSK flat vs. category
  • GSK growth slower than category

At the channel level:

  • 9+ Priority Markets growing faster
  • 6-8 Priority Markets growing faster
  • <6 Priority Markets growing faster

On Time In Full (OTIF)

Reported monthly

We always deliver to the customer what we say we will, at the agreed time

To count, an order must arrive to the customer both at the time agreed with nothing left out. At the moment it's calculated slightly differently across the portfolio:

OTIF Case Fill (monthly) - legacy GSK
OTIF Units Fill (monthly) - legacy Novartis

  • On target
  • Less than 2% off target
  • Greater than 2% off target

COGS

Reported monthly

Sustainable, incremental savings on Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

Percentage of Costs Of Goods Sold against Net Sales.

COGS includes production costs, variances and inventory write offs. We compare this to Net Sales which is our sales revenue less discounts and investment (e.g. rebates).

  • On target
  • Less than 0.5% missing target
  • Greater than 0.5 % missing target

Reliable Compliance

Reported monthly

A track record of continuous compliance where zero significant internal or regulatory compliance gaps are noted (all LOCs and Sites are in control) and where no negative business or reputational impacts occur as a result.

The total number of satisfactory internal audits (3-5 star rating or equivalent at LOC & Sites) and No Critical Findings & No Business Continuity Impact Outcomes divided by the total number of internal CAG and A&A audits and external regulatory agency inspections

The Inspection Audit Outcome metric is defined as:

  • 98-100%
  • 95%-98%
  • <95%
